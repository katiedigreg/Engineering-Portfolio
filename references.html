Embedded Source Extracts

The following extracts are drawn directly from my submitted Praxis I and Praxis II documentation, providing concrete evidence of my design methodology, quantitative testing, and cognitive development.

Source [4]: Process Analysis - Toulmin's Structure

"This observation helped me learn that engineering decisions require justification, not just intuition. I realized that even when something is intuitively reasonable, it is not actually valid in a design context unless I can explain why it is reasonable and how I know."

Portfolio Annotation

This extract directly supports my CTMF reflection on Toulmin's Structure of Argument in the Praxis I project. It marks the exact moment I transitioned from an intuition-based ("Dualistic") designer to an evidence-based engineer. It proves my understanding that a "Claim" without a "Warrant" is insufficient in professional design.

Source [5]: Final Exam Process Analysis - CRAAP Test

"This experience reshaped how I understand the design process by highlighting that credibility is not inherent—credibility must be earned, even when I am the source... This comparison taught me that part of the design process is determining which source—whether external or internal—provides the most defensible evidence, and that 'more credible' is often more important than 'perfect.'"

Portfolio Annotation

This extract validates my third Praxis I CTMF. It demonstrates my deep reflection on the nature of authority in engineering. Rather than blindly accepting poor secondary data or blindly trusting my own calculations, I learned to actively weigh the methodological transparency of primary research against the deficits of external sources.

Source [6]: Praxis II Design Booklet

"Our results showed that the system can apply a wristband in approximately 10.33 seconds, which is significantly faster than the current manual average of 22.7 seconds. For force and safety, we tested the applied pressure using a scale-based setup. The measured pressures ranged from approximately 3.24 to 7.25 kPa, all safely below the 10 kPa limit."

Portfolio Annotation

This extract provides the hard quantitative evidence used in the Praxis II "Measurement Matrix" CTMF section. It showcases my ability to not only build physical prototypes (overcoming my "analysis paralysis"), but to subject those prototypes to rigorous physical proxy testing to verify safety and efficiency requirements.

Source [6]: Praxis II Design Booklet

"Finally, after presenting our concept to the daycare, they confirmed that if the device is safe and reduces application time, they are open to implementing it. They also expressed interest in receiving the final design if it is fully usable."

Portfolio Annotation

This extract validates my final Praxis II CTMF on the Stakeholder Feedback Loop. It grounds my entire portfolio in my core "Volleyball Season" philosophy: that the ultimate goal of structuring disorder and moving through the FDCR process is to create a design that actually matters to the community.
